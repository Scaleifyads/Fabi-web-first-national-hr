Meet Our Founders

Executive HR leadership meets federal labor law enforcement expertise.

Fabiola Martinez

Cofounder

Fabiola Martinez is the Cofounder of First National Human Resources and brings over 15 years of Human Resources experience supporting manufacturing, distribution, and industrial organizations. She has extensive experience in talent acquisition, employee relations, workplace investigations, HR compliance, leadership coaching, performance management, and organizational development.

Originally from Veracruz, Mexico, Fabiola immigrated to the United States and built her career through hard work, dedication, and a passion for helping both employers and employees succeed. She is married and the proud mother of three children.

Throughout her career, Fabiola has partnered with business leaders to solve complex workplace challenges while creating compliant, productive, and people focused organizations. She believes that strong relationships, integrity, and practical solutions are the foundation of successful Human Resources.

Today, Fabiola is committed to helping businesses navigate employment laws, strengthen their workforce, and build workplaces where both companies and employees can thrive.

Oscar Blanco

Cofounder

Oscar Blanco was born in Venezuela to Dominican parents and immigrated to the United States at the age of eight. He is married and the proud father of four children.

His passion for public service began early in life, leading him to serve as a police officer before earning a Bachelor of Science in International Business and a Master of Business Administration (MBA).

Oscar later became a Federal Investigator, where he developed extensive expertise in federal Wage and Hour laws. Throughout his career, he recovered millions of dollars in unpaid wages for employees, received numerous awards and recognitions, and became an expert in labor compliance and workplace investigations.

Today, Oscar brings that same knowledge to employers, helping businesses proactively comply with federal labor laws, reduce legal risk, and avoid the types of investigations he once conducted.
